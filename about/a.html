The life of Van Gogh.

Van Gogh was a Dutch post-Impressionist artist. He is one of the most famous and influential figures in the history of Western art. His artworks include oil paintings, watercolors, drawings, and sketches. Van Gogh was born into an upper-middle-class family in 1853 in the village of Groot-Zundert in the province of North Brabant in the southern Netherlands. He was the eldest son of Theodorus van Gogh, a minister of the Dutch Reformed Church, and Anna Cornelia Carbentus. When he was just 16 years old, his father gave him a position at the art dealing firm Goupil & Cie in The Hague. This was a happy time for Vincent as he was able to explore his love for art without the pressure of having to produce saleable work. However, in 1876, after two years at Goupil's, Vincent was transferred to the company's branch in London, where he fell in love with his landlady's daughter, Elizabeth."Bessie" Reuters. Despite Vincent's objections, his father set him up with a position at the Paris branch of Goupil's in late 1873. Vincent was miserable in Paris and longed to return to the Netherlands. In 1879, after again becoming distraught over a love affair, Vincent quit his job at Goupil's and decided to become an artist.
